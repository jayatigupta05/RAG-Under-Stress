Transformers in Deep Learning

Overview:
Transformers are a neural network architecture introduced in 2017 in the paper "Attention Is All You Need". They are designed for processing sequential data, especially natural language.

Core Idea:
Transformers rely on a mechanism called self-attention, which allows the model to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence relative to each other.

Why Transformers Replaced RNNs:
- RNNs process tokens sequentially → slow and hard to parallelize
- Transformers process all tokens at once → faster training
- Better at capturing long-range dependencies

Key Components:

1. Self-Attention:
Each word attends to every other word in the sequence.
Example:
Sentence: "The cat sat on the mat"
The word "sat" attends to "cat" to understand who is performing the action.

2. Positional Encoding:
Since transformers process tokens in parallel, positional encoding adds information about word order.

3. Multi-Head Attention:
Multiple attention mechanisms run in parallel to capture different types of relationships.

4. Encoder-Decoder Architecture:
- Encoder: processes input
- Decoder: generates output

Applications:
- GPT (text generation)
- BERT (understanding tasks)
- Translation systems
- Chatbots

Limitations:
- High computational cost
- Requires large datasets

Summary:
Transformers are the foundation of modern large language models due to their ability to efficiently model relationships in data.
