Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Overview:
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances language models by combining them with external knowledge retrieval systems.

Problem with Standard LLMs:
- Trained on static datasets
- Cannot access real-time or private data
- May produce hallucinations (confident but incorrect answers)

Core Idea:
Instead of relying only on internal parameters, RAG retrieves relevant information from external documents and uses it during generation.

RAG Pipeline:

Step 1: Document Processing
Documents are split into smaller chunks and converted into embeddings.

Step 2: Storage
Embeddings are stored in a vector database.

Step 3: Retrieval
Given a query, the system finds the most relevant chunks using similarity search.

Step 4: Generation
The retrieved chunks are passed as context to a language model to generate the final answer.

Example Flow:
Query: "Why do transformers use attention?"
→ Retrieve chunk about self-attention
→ Feed chunk to LLM
→ Generate accurate answer

Advantages:
- Reduces hallucinations
- Improves factual grounding
- Enables domain-specific knowledge
- Works with private/company data

Challenges:
- Retrieval quality affects output
- Poor chunking leads to bad answers
- Latency increases due to retrieval step

Real-World Use Cases:
- Customer support chatbots
- Internal knowledge assistants
- Research assistants

Summary:
RAG makes language models more reliable by grounding them in external knowledge sources.
